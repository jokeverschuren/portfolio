This is me.

I'm an 18 year old graphic designer and web developer. Whyle trying to expres my creativity in my daily life, I spend my free time exploring the street art in Belgium. When I travel to foreign countries I let the street art conquer my heart.

PARIS

France - Mygalo

His paintings are influenced directly by everyday life, reflections on love or on the meaning of life. He also likes to nod to current affairs, and societal trends, or even advertisements in the form of parody or caricature. His interests tend to be separate to contemporary and street culture.

ANTWERP

Belgium - Dzia Krank

In his hometown of Antwerp, street artist Dzia is something of enigma – his animalistic murals pop up all over the city’s walls, breathing life into abandoned urban areas. It is more then just art, its something you will feel and see...it is raw emotion at its best and worst.

MILAN

Italy - SEA

He really likes to get inspiration from special places, it's important for his creativity to feel good and be always motivated by the places, so his characters can be more realistic, interactive and full of good vibrations.

A'DAM

The Netherlands - SHOE

Niels Shoe Meulman revolutionised the art of writing in 2007 with Calligraffiti, an art form that fuses calligraphy and graffiti. Since then, his calligraphic paintings have been shown in countless international exhibitions and are part of several museum collections. His recent painting style can be described as Abstract Expressionism with a calligraphic origin. It plays an important role in the new art movement Abstract Vandalism.

OAKLAND

California - Vogue

His ideal is to change people’s view of what graffiti is – or what you can do with a spraycan. He doesn’t think Americans know as a whole what can be done with this. He wants to prove that person wrong and give them another view, that when that word (graffiti) comes into their vocab they think of it differently.

MADRID

Spain - Beto

Beto (Alcorcón, Madrid, 1976) is one of those self-taught artists with graffiti background, who has worked conscientiously to develop his own characteristic style, based on the study of form, color mixing and how they affect lights and backlights on the characters that inhabit his particular imaginary world: “Betolandsky”.
